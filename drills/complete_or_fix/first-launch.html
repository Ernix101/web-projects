Birds site

This is where you can come to find any relevant information about certain birds, their species, where they're found and how they naturally behave

To get started, let's define them;

Birds:
They are a group of warm blooded vertebrates constituting the class Aves, characterised by feathers toothless beaked jaws, the laying of hard shell eggs, a high metabolic rate, a four chambered heart, and a strong yet lightweight skeleton

Birds live worldwide and range in size from 5.5cm (bee hummingbird) to the 2.8 m (common ostrich)

There are over 11,000 living species and are split into 44 orders.

BUT today let's look at a couple.

The Owl

Owls are nocturnal birds of prey (order stringiforms) found worldwide, known for their large heads, forward facing eyes, hooked beaks, sharp talons and silent flight thanks to specialized feathers

They are expert hunters, feeding on mammals, insects and birds and possess exceptional hearing and vision

The Crow

Crows are highly intelligent birds (family Corvidae) found worldwide, known for their adaptability and complex social behavior

They are opportunistic omnivores, feeding on a wide range of food including insects, small animals, fruits and human food waste

The ostrich

ostriches are large flightless birds (family Struthionidae) native to Africa, known for their long necks, powerful legs, and distinctive plumage

They are the largest living birds, with adults reaching up to 2.8 meters in height and weighing as much as 160 kg

The Penguin

penguins are flightless birds (family Spheniscidae) found primarily in the Southern Hemisphere, especially in Antarctica

They are adapted for life in the water, with streamlined bodies and strong flippers for swimming, and they primarily feed on fish, squid, and krill

The chicken

Chickens are domesticated birds (subspecies of the red junglefowl) that are raised worldwide for their eggs and meat

They are social animals that live in flocks and have a complex social hierarchy

The Parrot

Parrots are colorful birds (family Psittacidae) found in tropical and subtropical regions worldwide, known for their strong curved beaks, an upright stance, and strong legs

They are highly intelligent and social birds, often living in flocks and exhibiting complex behaviors
